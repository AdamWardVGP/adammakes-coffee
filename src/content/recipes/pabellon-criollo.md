I first found this dish from [Arepa Zone](https://www.arepazone.com/) when I lived in D.C. This became my staple for lunch 2-3 times per week because it was delicious. When I came back to CA I wanted to do some "meal prep" style dinners, and found this [Version from GOYA](https://www.goya.com/en/recipes/venezuelan-shredded-beef/) that I've since made a few modifications too.

---

## Shopping List

### For Pabellón
- 2+ lb flank steak 
- 1/2 onion, for sauce
- 1/2 onion, for beef
- 1 red bell pepper
- 6 garlic clove
- 4 tsp beef bouillon
- 1 quart water
- 2 tbsp corn oil
- 1 can (12 oz) tomato sauce
- 1.5 tsp sazón (no annatto)
- 1 tsp oregano
- 1 tsp adobo 
- 1 tsp cumin

### For Beans
- 1 can black beans
- 1/2 lime
- 1 tsp cumin
- 2 tsp lard
- Cotija cheese

I get ~4-5 servings out of this depending on how much I take when I go back for seconds. You will go back for seconds.

> I don't make plantains (can't find them), but I do make rice. Make sure you do that too.

---

## Prep

- Dice the onion - 1/2"
- Rough chop the red pepper
- Peel garlic
- Cut the flank steak into 1" wide strips.

---

## Beef

- Add flank steak, 1/2 the chopped onion, bouillon, and water to a big ol' pot. (We're gonna use the same pot twice)
- Bring to boil, then lower heat to simmer until beef is very tender (~1.5 hours).
- Reserve 1 cup of the broth, take out the beef, then I usually strain the rest of the broth to get the onions back.
- Shred beef into thin strands.

---

## Sauce time

- In a food processor pulse the chopped onion, red pepper, and garlic, and the other onion that
was just cooked. Goal is small 1/8" pieces, not a puree.
- Wipe out whatever is left in the pot, add some oil, and heat to med/high.
- Add shredded beef and lightly brown.
- Add the veggie puree, tomato sauce, all the spices, and reserved broth.
- Stir it all together, bring to a simmer and cook until the mixture reduces (~20 min) meanwhile start the sides.

Taste and adjust salt as needed. 

---

## Rice

Follow your own directions for rice.

---

## Black Beans

- Drain the beans and save a little bit of the sauce in the can.
- Heat black beans in a small pot.
- In this I juice half the lime, add a bit of salt, the cumin, and 2 tsp of lard. stir.

---

## Get served

Just put the rice on the bottom, beans and meat on the side. Top the beans with a generous sprinkling of Cotija. Then add even more Cotija, or don't but I recommend it.
